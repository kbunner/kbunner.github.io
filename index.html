Hi There,

Originally from Nebraska, I moved to California to attend Stanford University. While at Stanford I studied Computer Science, with a focus on information and data. This track utilizes techniques like machine learning and data mining and applies it to a variety of areas, such as data models, security, and NLP. Through internship experience and classroom projects, I have applied my computing knowledge to projects ranging from a web tool focused on the revitalization of the Yup’ik Language to implementing reinforcement learning algorithms to effectively play the card game skull. I have tried to detail some of my favorite projects on the Projects page, and have included GitHub repositories where I can.
I also pursued a minor in Native American studies, which offered a great balance to my technical coursework. The small class sizes of Native Studies courses provided an intimate environment and required active participation for discussion of the material. The minor enhanced my ability effectively articulate and defend complex topics and gave me perspective on issues that impact contemporary Natives.
Outside of academics, I like to keep busy. At Stanford, I was actively involved with the Stanford Native Community. I held a leadership position in Stanford’s chapter of the American Indian Society of Engineers and Scientists, including President during my senior year. I served as the Financial Officer of the 2016 Powwow (one of the largest powwow’s in the country). I worked for the School of Engineering Diversity Office, coordinating Stanford’s only job fair dedicated to diversity in STEM. I was an active member of Stanford Tridelta, and held leadership positions there.
Otherwise, you can probably find me scrolling through the Twitter pages of many of NASA’s missions, playing boardgames with friends, like Santorini and Splendor, or baking (gf) banana bread. I also like to be active and exploring new things. My favorite ways to do this are backpacking in our National Parks with friends or following the advice of Where to Go When and traveling to new places.
