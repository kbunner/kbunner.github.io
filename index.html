Software Projects

Below you will find descriptions of some of my favorite projects. I have included GitHub links where I can. My preferred language is Python, and I have a fair amount of experience with Java, JavaScript, HTML, C++ and SQL. I have also worked with Julia, C, Android, and Google Apps Scipts before.

    Looking for ways to combine my technical skills and my passion for Native Communities, I have been a part of two projects that have focused on language revitalization efforts for Indigenous languages. Continuing to develop tools that are community focused and culturally aware is a huge part of my career aspirations.

  • Web Development

    My first experience building a web application came from the neccessity of needing to showcase my senior project. Since then I have taken more courses, like Web Development and Computer Security, as well as completed side projects, like this site, to further develop my ability to program web applications.

  • Systems Programming

    Stanford ensures that their engineering graduates are well-rounded and require all Computer Science majors to take at least two systems classes, giving us an understanding of how computer systems execute programs and manipulate data, working from the C programming language down to the microprocessor. My final project was a complex Map Reduce system that took advantage of parrallel computing. While this is not an area I have a strong desire to continue working in, I am thankful to have a strong background in understanding how machines works.

  • Android

    Learning to use Android Studio definitely had a bit of a learning curve, but it was an area I am glad I have exposure to. Through a course on Object Oriented Systems Design, I completed 8 Android Applications, mostly for game playing. The largest was a multi-class game player and editor developed with a team of 3 other students. This work gave me a strong appreciation for people who do UI/UX work and gave me a great experience working with a team to build a software project.
